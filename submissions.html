Submissions

Digital Assignments Guidelines

  1. Form a Team: Each team must consist of 1 or 2 members, depending on the complexity of the selected topic.
  2. Topic Selection: You are free to choose a topic of interest from the following areas:
    • Core Computer Networks.
    • Advanced topics in networking.
    • Real-world problems related to any topic.
    • Topics under the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
  3. Activity-Based Learning Submission: Choose one activity from the Activity-Based Learning section and align your submission with the selected topic.
  4. Refine Your Topic: After topic selection, narrow it down to a specific subtopic, subdomain, or research area.
  5. Problem Identification: Identify a real-world problem within your chosen domain and propose a suitable and meaningful title for your project.
  6. Proposal Approval Process: Complete steps 1â€“5 and submit your proposal using the given template on Microsoft Teams. Wait for my review. Based on the relevance and uniqueness of the topic:
    • Your proposal will either be approved or rejected.
    • Upon approval, your team name, project title, and assignment type will be published on our course website.
    • Only after publication can your team officially proceed with the project.
  7. Ensure Uniqueness: Each project must have a unique problem statement. Duplicate or copied ideas will be rejected.
  8. Assignment Deadlines: Deadlines are strict and final.
    • No extensions will be granted for any reason (including events, holidays, or exams).
    • If you anticipate a scheduling conflict, ensure you submit in advance.
    • No excuses will be entertained under any circumstances.
  9. Where to submit: You have to submit your assignment in the (moodle) lms.
  10. Assignment template: You can scroll down to know the assignment template details.

Activity-Based Learning

  1. Real-World Problems: Design practical solutions for real-life issues such as traffic management, waste recycling, air and water pollution. You may develop prototype models or create algorithms as part of your solution.
  2. Industry Simulations: Use benchmark datasets, real-world data, or case studies from actual companies to analyze challenges and propose effective solutions.
  3. Innovation Sprints: Build a working prototype to address a specific problem using any available tools, kits, or materials.
  4. Field Assignments: Visit nearby sites to observe and analyze smart city technologies in action, such as IoT devices, traffic management systems, or renewable energy solutions.
  5. Digital Storytelling: Create engaging narratives on smart city concepts or syllabus-related topics using platforms like Canva, Prezi, or other storytelling tools.
  6. Teaching Peers: Choose a topic aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and deliver it creatively to school students using videos, comics, or games. (Note: Only one team will be allowed for this activity, granted on a first-come, first-served basis. As the audience primarily speaks Tamil, at least one Tamil-speaking student must be part of the team.)
  7. Short Films: Create impactful short films related to Smart Cities or SDG topics to raise awareness or offer solutions.
  8. Cross-Disciplinary Assignments: Collaborate with other departments to combine diverse perspectivesâ€”for example, integrating smart city design with environmental science or public policy.
  9. SDG Topics: Any of the above activities can be aligned with relevant Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
  10. Others: If you wish to bring in any new concept or idea for the submission, you are most welcome.
